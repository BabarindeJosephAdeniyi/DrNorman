Dr. Norman Borlaug


The man who saves a million lives


Dr. Norman Borlaug, third from the left, trains biologist in Mexico on how to increase wheat yields-part of his life-long war on hunger.


Here's a timeline of Dr. Borlaug's life;



  • 1913 - Born in Cresco, Lowa
  • 1933 - Leaves his family farm to attend the University of Minnesoca, thanks to a depression era program know as "National Youth Administration"
  • 1935 - He has to stop school and save up more money Civillan Conservations Corps, helping starving Americans "I saw how food changed them",he said,"All of this left scars on me."
  • 1937 - Finishes University and takes a job in U.S forestry Service
  • 1938 - Marries wife or age 69 Margret Gibbson. Get laids off due to bugdet. Inspired by Elvins Charles Stakman, he returns to school study under Stakman who teach him about breeding pest-resistant plants
  • 1941 - Tries to enroll in the military after Pearl Harbor attack,but is rejecte. Instead the military has his lab to work on waterproof glue,DDT to control malaria, disinfectants and other applied Service
,
  • 1942 - Receives Ph.D in Genetics and plants Pathology
  • 1944 - Rejects 100% salary increase from Dupont, leaves behind his pregnant wife and flies to Mexico to head a new plant Pathology program. Over the next 16 years, his team breeds 6000 different strains disease resistent wheat-including different varieties for each major climate on earth
  • 1945 - Discover a way to grown wheat twice each season doubling wheat yields
  • 1953 - Crosses a short, sturdy dwaf breed of wheat with a high-yielding Americans breed creating a strain that respond to well fertilizer. It goes on to provides 95% of Mexico wheat.
  • 1962 - visits Delhi and bring hys high-yielding strains of wheat to India sobcontnent in time to help mitigate mass starvation due to rapidly expanding population
  • 1970 - 1970-Receives the Nobel Peae prize
  • 1983 - Helps seven African countries dramatically increase their maize and sorghum yields
  • 1984 - Becomes a distinguished Professor at Texas A&M University
  • 2005 - States "we will have ti double the world food supply at 2050." Argues that genetically modified crops are the only way to meet the demands as we run out of arable lands.
